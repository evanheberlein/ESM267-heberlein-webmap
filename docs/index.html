EROSION MODELING IN THE CABY REGION

The Bren School Group Project Forests to Faucets is working with American Rivers to reduce post-fire erosion risk in the central Sierra Nevada mountains of California. This project is focused on the Cosumnes, American, Bear, and Yuba (CABY) watersheds on the west slope of the Sierras. Forests to Faucets' main objective is recommending locations for controlled burns to lower fire severity and risk in sensitive areas of the CABY region, ultimately reducing negative impacts to water quality via post-fire erosion.

Optimally locating controlled burns requires knowledge of where erosion potential is high in the CABY region. The Watershed Erosion Prediction Project (WEPP) model is being used to project areas of high erosivity. The map below demonstrates how WEPP plots watersheds based on a digital elevation model.
